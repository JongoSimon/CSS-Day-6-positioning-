Explanation:
Absolute positioning places an element relative to its nearest positioned ancestor (an ancestor with a `position` value other than `static`). In this example, the child is absolutely positioned and placed 30px from the top and 50px from the left of the container. This works because the container has `position: relative; , which establishes the positioning context for the child. If the container did not have a `position` value other than `static`, the child would be positioned relative to the viewport instead.
